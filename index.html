Go-Forth Emotional Intelligence Assessment

At Go-Forth Companies, we believe that great leadership begins with self-awareness. That’s why we created this Emotional Intelligence (EQ) Assessment—to help you better understand how you lead, how you respond under pressure, and how you connect with others.

This isn’t a pass/fail test—it’s a growth tool designed to give you a clear snapshot of your strengths and reveal areas for development. As we continue to build a culture rooted in accountability, empathy, and excellence, your insights from this assessment will help guide your personal development journey—and support the people you lead.

Please answer honestly, based on how you typically think, feel, and act day to day. Your results will help inform future leadership training, coaching conversations, and your individual growth path at Go-Forth.

Let’s grow together.

Instructions: Rate each statement from 1 (Strongly Disagree) to 5 (Strongly Agree).

DOMAIN 1: Know Yourself

Self-awareness of emotions, strengths, and blind spots.

1. When I receive unexpected feedback, my first instinct is to stay curious, not defensive.
2. I can usually name the emotion I'm feeling in real time.
3. When things go sideways, I can pinpoint what part I played.
4. I know what kinds of stress make me shut down or overreact.
5. People have described me as self-aware or reflective.
6. Before I act, I often take a moment to check in with myself.
7. I regularly reflect on how my mood affects my decision-making.

Scenario A:

A teammate gives you critical feedback on something you thought went well. What do you most likely do?

Scenario B:

You realize you're feeling unusually frustrated during a routine task. What's your first move?

DOMAIN 2: Stay Steady

Self-regulation and emotional control under pressure.

1. In tense situations, I tend to stay calm and composed.
2. If someone challenges me in a meeting, I respond thoughtfully.
3. I can control my tone and body language even when irritated.
4. When someone pushes my buttons, I usually respond rather than react.
5. During high pressure, I rely on routines or techniques to stay grounded.
6. I can stay focused even when things feel chaotic around me.
7. I have strategies to stay centered when things get overwhelming.

Scenario A:

A client sends an angry email blaming your team for a mistake. What’s your typical reaction?

Scenario B:

You’re juggling back-to-back meetings and someone brings up a new urgent issue. How do you respond?

DOMAIN 3: Own It

Personal responsibility, drive, and initiative.

1. I take initiative even when it's not expected of me.
2. When something doesn’t get done, I check my part first.
3. I follow through on tasks without needing reminders.
4. I push myself to improve, even without external rewards.
5. If I fall short, I admit it and work to fix it.
6. I take pride in doing my best, even when no one's watching.
7. I take full responsibility for my actions, even when it’s uncomfortable.

Scenario A:

You miss a key deadline that affects another team. What’s your instinctive response?

Scenario B:

Your manager is out for the week. There’s an issue you could either handle or escalate. What do you do?

DOMAIN 4: Read the Room

Empathy, awareness of others, and emotional insight.

1. I can usually tell how others are feeling, even if they don't say it.
2. If someone’s having a bad day, I tend to notice right away.
3. I try to understand where others are coming from before responding.
4. People feel comfortable opening up to me.
5. I pick up on non-verbal cues (body language, tone, etc.) easily.
6. I can adapt how I speak depending on who I'm talking to.
7. I seek feedback on how my actions impact others.

Scenario A:

You notice someone in a meeting seems unusually quiet. What do you do?

Scenario B:

You're in a group conversation where tension seems to be rising. What’s your go-to move?

DOMAIN 5: Connect & Influence

Relationship-building, communication, and leadership presence.

1. I can build rapport quickly with new people.
2. When giving feedback, I consider how the person might take it.
3. I know how to lead people without relying on authority.
4. I help bring people together when there’s conflict.
5. I can influence others without being pushy.
6. I intentionally build trust with those I work with.
7. I make a conscious effort to build trust with those I lead.

Scenario A:

You're asked to lead a team of peers with mixed opinions on direction. What’s your first step?

Scenario B:

You need to give constructive feedback to a teammate who is defensive. What’s your strategy?
